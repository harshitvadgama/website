Who's helping

Automotive engineering student, hands-on with the real tools

I'm not reading this off a manual. I use these tools on actual cars.

I'm studying Fahrzeugtechnik (automotive engineering) and spend most of my free time doing what the coursework only theorizes about: reading fault codes, coding control units, and tracing wiring faults on real cars.

I've worked hands-on in independent diagnostics, done high-voltage battery production work on the OEM side, and spent plenty of late nights on forums in three languages chasing down one obscure coding parameter. If your car is giving you a code nobody can explain, there's a decent chance I've already chased something similar.

This isn't a business. It's free — I do it because I like solving these problems, and because most car owners get quoted a diagnostic fee before anyone even looks at the car.
